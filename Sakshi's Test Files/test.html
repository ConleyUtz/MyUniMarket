Purdue University is a public research university in West Lafayette, Indiana, and the flagship campus of the Purdue University system. The university was founded in 1869 after Lafayette businessman John Purdue donated land and money to establish a college of science, technology, and agriculture in his name.

Fast Facts About Purdue University

HOME TO:
  1. Three of the world’s TOP500 supercomputers (Rice, Conte, and Carter).
  2. 23 NASA astronauts – including the first and most recent men on the moon.
  3. The largest university-affiliated incubation park in the U.S.
  4. 4 Nobel Prize Laureates, 2 World Food Prize Laureates, and 3 National Medal of Technology and Innovation Laureates.
IN ADDITION, PURDUE:
  1. Ranks fourth in the nation among top public universities in the U.S. (Wall Street Journal).
  2. Has more than 400 study abroad opportunities for students in 60 countries.
  3. Conducts more than 2,000 undergraduate research projects across campus each year.
  4. Is a welcoming campus, with Purdue among only 7% of U.S. colleges with an LGBTQ Center. Campus Pride Index has awarded Purdue an LGBTQ campus rating of 4/5 stars.
  5. Has generated 131 licensing deals, 178 U.S. and global patents, and 25 start-ups. The Big 10 average number of start-ups is 6.
  6. Is located in an excellent place to live and work—Forbes ranked Greater Lafayette as the #2 best small place for business and careers.
